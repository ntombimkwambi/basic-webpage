Historic places in Cape Town

1.The Castle of Good Hope

  • It is said that the first stone to build this castle was laid on the 2nd of January in 1666 and it was completed by April 1679.
  • The castle was built by soldiers, volunteers, slaves and Khoi undergoing panishment.
  • The castle was once called "Kui keip" which means (stone kraal) and sometimes "Citadei" by Dutch settlers.

2.Parliament of RSA

  • Before 1910, the Parliament Buildings housed the bicameral Parliament of the Cape of Good Hope which functioned as the legislature of the Cape.
  • There are 13 different political parties that are currently being represented with the African National Congress (ANC) being the majority party.
  • Every 5 years the people of South Africa are given a chance to cast their votes for the new parliament.

3.Iziko Slave Lodge

  • It is said that the Iziko Slave Lodge building is one of the oldest buildings in Cape Town.
  • Over three centuries, this building has had three name changes- from Government Offices Building, Old Supreme Court and SA cultural History Museum.
  • The Slave Lodge has two levels- the upper level and the lower level which explores the long history of Slavery of South Africa
